Scriptation

Overview

Scriptation is a unique PDF text editor that is aimed at the Hollywood movie industry. Scriptation was a big hit on the iOS platform and had received praise from Apple for their innovation. Scriptation decided to bring their product to the Windows platform and ecosystem.

I was asked to give design guidance for about a day but ended up designing massive files for their development team and working closely with the product owner on creating a delightful user experience by taking advantage of the technology Microsoft has to offer. Below are designs of this product and a small glimpse of the process used to get to the final design.

Applying Fluent Design

After having laid down a solid UX structure in the wireframing phase I started to design out each screen. Scriptation being one of the very first few apps that adopted fluent design so early I was entering unknown grounds. Implementing a new design language was challenging, thrilling, motivating, rewarding and extremely insightful.

Input devices

Windows supports many input methods that are extremely powerful when tailored to the right experience. In addition to surface pen inking I designed a user interface for the surface dial to accommodate the pen and ultimately boost user task efficiency. As simple as the dial might look the UX behind it is more complex than some may think.

Motion

Motion can be a powerful element that can guide a user through the app experience. I've also done experiements with motion and how best we can leverage this element to make the app I am desiging that much better.

Wireframing

I spend much time prototyping my wireframes and sharing with stakeholder in order for us all to get a better idea of what feels right and where we can improve the user experience. At this phase getting the UX right was extremely importuning.

Prototyping wireframes using Adobe XD

Adobe XD is a fantastic tool for quick wireframing, an excellent tool to show interactions and page flow. Below is one video of a few wireframe prototypes shared with stakeholders and the development team in the early stages of this project.

Redlines / Specs

My design process didn’t just end with polished designs, I worked really hard to make sure my designs were implemented right and on time. I believe working close with the development is a must for any designer and understanding development limitations early on in the product design cycle will help you make better design decisions…also your developers won’t hate you :)
